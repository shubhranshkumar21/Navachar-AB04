The Mauryan Empire

Year 8 of the Reign of Chakravarthy Ashoka


Let it be known across the expanse of Jambudvipa, from the snow-covered peaks of the Himalayas to the coastal lands kissed by the Great Southern Waters, that the Mauryan Empire stands in its full glory — vast, wise, and just under the benevolent eye of Emperor Ashoka, the Beloved of the Gods.

Our lineage springs from Chandragupta Maurya, founder of this mighty realm, who with the guidance of the wise Chanakya, cast off foreign yoke and forged unity among the Mahajanapadas. In his valor, he laid the first stone of empire, upon which now rises a dominion bound not by chains, but by Dharma and righteous order.

Bindusara, son of Chandragupta and father to Ashoka, expanded the borders with firmness and vision, ensuring the Mauryan name echoed in every court and market across Aryavarta and beyond.

Now in these blessed times, His Majesty Ashoka rules not merely by sword, but by the law of compassion. Since the conquest of Kalinga — a battle that shook the soul of our sovereign — he has renounced bloodshed and taken up the banner of Dharma. Pillars of stone and caves carved with his edicts proclaim his message to man and beast alike: live rightly, treat all with kindness, uphold truth.

Our roads stretch like veins across the land, bringing trade, wisdom, and pilgrims to the farthest provinces. The royal post rides swift as the wind. Physicians are dispatched, shade trees are planted, wells dug — for His Majesty cares for all his subjects, two-legged and four.

O people of the Mauryan soil — artisans, scholars, warriors, monks — take pride in the empire you shape with your hands and hearts. Let our children's children remember this golden age as one where Dharma prevailed, and a man called Ashoka chose peace over conquest, yet ruled an empire unmatched in size, spirit, and wisdom.


May the wheel of Dharma ever turn.

Long live the Sammrat.
